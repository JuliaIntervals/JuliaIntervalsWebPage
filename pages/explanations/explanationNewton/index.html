    Introduction

    This page will explain you how to generalize the traditional Newton method for root-finding to deal with intervals. Basically, here you will get a taste of what happens under the hood in the IntervalRootFinding when the newton method is used.

    Review: traditional Newton method

    It is well know from high-school or undergraduate studies that given an initial guess x0x_0x0​ for the root of a function fff, the root can be found iteratively with the upgrade rule

    xk+1=xk−f(xk)f′(xk), x_{k+1} = x_k - \frac{f(x_k)}{f'(x_k)}, xk+1​=xk​−f′(xk​)f(xk​)​,

    which is known as Newton method. Geometrically, the Newton method is equivalent to the following geometrical construction, visualized in the animation below

    1. Draw the tangent to fff passing through the point (xk,f(xk))(x_k, f(x_k))(xk​,f(xk​))

    2. The new point xk+1x_{k+1}xk+1​ will be the root of the tangent line.
